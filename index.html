Explainables for AI

An introductory course designed by Hendrik Strobelt and David Bau

Topics to chose from

  1. Pre-processing: What are good steps for pre-processing? Which tools help with this task? What influence has pre-processing on model training? Show influence.
  2. Methods for prediction visualization - black box methods: Find examples in VIS literature. Explain two methods using their demo systems. Create an own small demo system for a (text or image) classifier and explain insights.
  3. Adversarial Attacks in Computer Vision How to detect AA?, how to mitigate AA?, Is adversarial robustness === interpretability method? Play with detection and mitigation methods.
  4. Latent Variables in Computer Vision Latent Var walks? What is the difference between latent vars of a VAE vs. vanilla GAN vs. StyleGAN, LatentCompass, Philipp Isola (steerability in GANs, GANalyze) – play with latent walks and steerable GANs
  5. Saliency Methods (2 students): What can we read from them? Is there any difference between methods (chose 3 at least)? What do they cover, what not ? What is Shared Interest? How to benchmark SM? ( see Julius Adebaio’s work
  6. Network/GAN Dissection How does it work? What could it reveal? What are the limitations? Any good examples?
  7. Dimensionality Reduction methods - Are they useful? Is there a difference using projections for pre-processing vs. post-hoc embeddings? PCA, TSNE, UMAP,...
  8. Attention in Transformers - What can we see ? What is hidden? What do they uncover? (warning: might be advanced, because simple idea is discussed in class)
  9. Lime and SHAP Local Linear explanation methods. Explain and demonstrate their use. Are they any good ?
  10. BLEU / Evaluating Generative NLP Models What is BLEU? Why would we use it? What is it bad at capturing? What are alternative methods? (see: gem-benchmark.com)
  11. FID / Semantic FID / Evaluating Generative Image Models: see BLEU for Computer Vision
  12. Fairness methods (maybe 2 students)- What’s fairness? Why do we need it? Group vs Individual fairness? How to detect fairness issues? How to mitigate them? Demonstrate detection and mitigation prototype (Warning: do not underestimate!)
